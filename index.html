A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

"Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action."

Early Life

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born on 15th October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He was raised in a humble background, with his father being a boat owner and imam of a local mosque. From a young age, Kalam was fascinated by the mechanics of flying and dreamt of becoming a pilot. Despite financial struggles, he pursued his education passionately and developed a keen interest in mathematics and physics.

Career

After graduating in aerospace engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT), Kalam joined the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO). He initially worked on developing a small hovercraft but found his true calling at the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO). There, he led the project to build India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite into orbit in 1980.

Kalam’s dedication and vision earned him the title "Missile Man of India" for his work on developing ballistic missile technology. He returned to DRDO later to contribute to the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program. His leadership in various defense and space programs cemented his reputation as an influential scientist.

Achievements and Presidency

Dr. Kalam’s contributions to science and technology were recognized with numerous awards and accolades. He was honored with the Padma Bhushan in 1981, the Padma Vibhushan in 1990, and the Bharat Ratna, India’s highest civilian award, in 1997. His presidency from 2002 to 2007 was marked by his continued commitment to inspiring youth and advocating for innovation and self-reliance.

During his tenure as President of India, he was affectionately known as the "People's President" due to his approachable demeanor and dedication to the welfare of the public. His vision for India's development was embodied in "India 2020," a roadmap to transform the country into a superpower through advancements in science, technology, and economic growth.

Legacy and Inspirational Impact

Dr. Kalam’s legacy extends far beyond his scientific achievements. He was an inspirational figure who dedicated his life to empowering the youth of India through education and mentorship. His book "Wings of Fire," an autobiography, continues to inspire millions, detailing his journey from a humble background to becoming a key figure in India’s space and defense sectors.

He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, leaving behind a legacy of humility, resilience, and an unyielding spirit of innovation. Dr. Kalam's life and teachings remain a guiding light for individuals striving for excellence and service to the nation.
